Privacy Policy — Scnr

Last updated: April 21, 2026

Scnr (“the app”) is a document scanner that runs entirely on your device. This policy explains what information the app processes, what it does not collect, and which third-party services are involved.

1. What stays on your device

Everything you create inside Scnr is stored locally on your phone:

  • Scanned images and generated PDFs
  • OCR text extracted from your scans
  • Signatures you draw inside the app
  • Folder passwords (stored in the OS secure keystore)
  • App preferences (theme, language, etc.)

None of this data is uploaded to any server, cloud, or backend. The app does not have a user account system, and there is no sync to any remote service.

2. Permissions the app requests

  • Camera — used only to capture documents and barcodes. Images are processed on-device and saved to the app’s private storage. They are never transmitted off the device.
  • Photo library (iOS) / Storage (Android) — used only when you choose to import a file or export a PDF. Access is limited to the specific file you pick.
  • Biometrics (Face ID / fingerprint) — used only to unlock folders you have protected with a password. Biometric data never leaves the OS secure enclave.

3. On-device processing

Optical character recognition (OCR) and document edge detection run on-device using Apple Vision (iOS) and Google ML Kit (Android). These frameworks perform inference locally; Scnr does not send your images to any server for analysis.

4. Third-party services

Scnr integrates with a small number of third-party SDKs that may process limited data:

RevenueCat (subscription management)

When you purchase Scnr Premium, RevenueCat handles the subscription lifecycle. RevenueCat receives an anonymous, app-generated identifier together with the purchase receipt issued by Apple or Google. No personal information (name, email, etc.) is provided to RevenueCat by Scnr. See their privacy policy: revenuecat.com/privacy.

Google AdMob (free tier only)

If you use the free version, Scnr shows banner and interstitial ads provided by Google AdMob. AdMob may collect limited information (advertising identifier, device model, coarse region) to serve and measure ads. Scnr requests non-personalized ads by default. Premium users see no ads, and no ad SDK activity occurs for them. See Google’s ads privacy policy: policies.google.com/technologies/ads.

On iOS 14.5+, Scnr asks for App Tracking Transparency consent before any tracking identifier is used. You can withdraw this consent at any time from iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking.

Apple / Google payment platforms

Subscription payments are processed by Apple (App Store) or Google (Google Play). Scnr does not see or store your payment details. Refer to Apple’s and Google’s privacy policies for how they handle transaction data.

5. Analytics

Scnr does not integrate any analytics SDK. We do not track screen views, taps, usage time, or any other behavioural metric.

6. Children

Scnr is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

7. Data retention and deletion

Because Scnr stores your data locally, you control retention. Deleting a document inside the app removes it from your device. Uninstalling the app deletes all remaining Scnr data from your device.

If you cancel your Premium subscription, RevenueCat retains the purchase record in accordance with its policy. You may request deletion by contacting the developer.
